°  °  °  es• >svpath -h

@ 'svpath' should be used in conjunction with the 'cd' command. @

> svpath [choice] [argument]
> svpath [-s, -rm, -d, -ls, -lsp, -h]
> svpath [argument] to temporarily 
.  .  .  store the current path as a given
.  .  .  argument (as you want).
°  °  °  es • > svpath home
> svpath [-s] [argument] to permanently
.  .  .  store the current path as a given
.  .  .  argument (the path is saved in a
.  .  .  file).
°  °  °  es• > svpath -s home
> svpath [-rm] [argument] to remove the
.  .  .  permanent path associated with
.  .  .  the argument.
> svpath [-d] [argument] to delete the
.  .  .  temporary path associated whit
.  .  .  the argument.
> svpath [-lsp] to list the permanent 
.  .  .  saved path.
> svpath [-ls] to list the temporary 
.  .  .  saved path.
> svpath [-h] to visualize this page.
